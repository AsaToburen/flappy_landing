  • The Rise of Flappy Bird

    In May 2013, a vietnam-based game called Flappy Bird was released to app stores. The game involved guiding a small bird through rows of green pipes. The popularity of the game spiked. Some reports indicated that the game had grossed as much as $50,000 in daily revenue.

  • Ruffled Feathers

    Soon after the game was released, there were accusations of plagiarism and criticism that the game was too difficult and addictive. In February 2014, the creator of the game, Dong Nguyen, elected to remove the game from app stores due to overuse and the game's addictive nature.

  • The Bird Flies Again

    Fortunately for you, countless clones of the game are available online. Also, the creator of the game has stated that a alternate version of the game might be released to app stores in the future. The next release is said to include a multiplayer version and would be less habit forming.
